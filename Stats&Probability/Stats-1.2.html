Basic Theoretical Probability

Flipping a coin has 50% probability of Heads, P(H) = 1/2

# dice rolling a 1 on 6 sided die = f/n 
prob = function(f, n){
  p = f/n
  glue('Probability is {round(p, 2)*100}%')
}

prob(2,6) # probability of rolling a 1 or 6
## Probability is 33%
prob(3,6) # probability of rolling an even
## Probability is 50%

Sample space

Find the probability of pulling a yellow marble from a bag with 3 yellow, 2 red, 2 green and 1 blue

possible_outcomes = c(
  rep('Y', times=3),
  rep('R', times=2),
  rep('G', times=2),
  rep('B',1)
)

n = length(possible_outcomes)

prob(3, n)
## Probability is 38%

Bag of marbles: 9 red, 2 blue, 3 green in it. What is the probability of selecting a non-blue marble?

bag = c( 
  rep('R', times=9, each=1),
  rep('B', times=2, each=1),
  rep('G', times= 3)
)

n = length(bag)
# non blue marble 14-2
prob(12, n)
## Probability is 86%

A number is randomly chosen, what is the probability that number is a multiple of 5?

nums = c(32,49,55,30,56,28,50,40,40,45,3,25)

n = length(nums)

# divide the vector by 5, use modulus to get only the ones with 0
f = sum(nums%%5 == 0)

prob(f,n)
## Probability is 58%

Conditional Probability

BAYES THEOREM = P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B)

Probability of event A is 0.6, probability of event B is 0.5, probability of A given B is 0.7. Find P(B | A).

P(A and B) = P(A | B) * P(B) = P(B|A) * P(A)

A_given_B = 0.7
B = 0.5
A = 0.6

A_and_B = A_given_B * B

glue("P(A and B) = {A_and_B}")
## P(A and B) = 0.35
# A * P(B | A) = 0.35
B_given_A = A_and_B / A
glue("P(B given A) = {round(B_given_A,2)}")
## P(B given A) = 0.58
A = 0.55
B = 0.18
AandB = 0.05

BgivenA = AandB/A 
glue("P(B|A) = {round(BgivenA,4)}")
## P(B|A) = 0.0909
A_given_B = AandB/B
glue("P(A|B) = {round(A_given_B,4)}")
## P(A|B) = 0.2778
